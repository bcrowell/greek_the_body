-------- seat of emotions or faculties -----------
ἦτορ, ἤτορος,n3 - heart, 93 (gen unattested in homer, only sing nom & acc occur)
σπλάγχνα,σπλάγχνων,n2 - 9, guts, seat of feelings (only in plural; famous biblical issue)
στῆθος,στήθεος,n3 - breast, chest, seat of masculine feelings such as bravery, 191
στέρνον,στέρνοιο,n2 - breast, chest, seat of feelings, 19 (for men much more commonly στῆθος)
φρήν,φρενός,f3 - midriff, mind, 308
πραπίδες,πραπίδων,f3 - diaphragm, midriff, seat of wisdom and cunning, 11 (only in plural in Homer, and indexed by perseus under plural)
ὦμος,ὤμου,m2 - shoulder, 161 (thought of as the seat of strength; cog humerus)


-------- only in plural ---------
μῆδεα,μηδέων - genitals, testicles 23 (indexed by perseus as the singular μῆδος; is this only male?)
πλευρά,πλευρᾶς,f1 - rib, 5 (only in plural)

-------- plurals different from English ---------
ῥί_ς,ῥι_νός,f3 - nose, nostrils, 15 (the plural can mean the nose, i.e., it's thought of as an organ in two halves)
μαζός,μαζοῖο,m2 - male or female nipple, female breast, plural is male chest, 17 (indexed by perseus under μαστός)

-------- never mentioned except in compounds ---------
δάκτυλος - finger, toe (only in H in rhododactylos heos; there is no separate word for thumb)
παρήϊον,παρήϊου,n2 - cheek, 5 [text has diar, perseus omits it from lemma; Also occurs as παρήιον, without the diaresis. Homer frequently uses the epithet καλλιπάρῃος, fair-cheeked, for women and goddesses. Attic παρειά.]
ὠλένη,ὠλένης,f1 - elbow, lower arm (seems to occur only in the epithet λευκώλενος)


-------- divided differently than in English -----------

κνήμη,κνήμης,f1 - calf, lower leg, cf. ἐϋκνήμιδες 16
μηρός,μηροῦ,m2 - thigh, 36
μέλος,μέλεος,m2 - limb, 24
